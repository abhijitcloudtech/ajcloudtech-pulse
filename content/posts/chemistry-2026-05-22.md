## AI Pioneers a New Era in "Forever Chemical" Remediation

**May 22, 2026** – In a significant leap for environmental chemistry, a groundbreaking collaboration between Kemira and CuspAI has leveraged artificial intelligence to engineer novel materials capable of removing per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS), commonly known as "forever chemicals," from drinking and process water. This breakthrough marks a pivotal moment in the fight against widespread water contamination.

PFAS are a group of synthetic chemicals that have been used in various industrial and consumer products for decades. Their persistence in the environment and potential health risks have made their remediation a global challenge. Traditional methods for removing these resilient compounds are often energy-intensive or not fully effective at trace concentrations.

The recent innovation centers on the use of generative AI to design new Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs). These highly porous crystalline materials possess tunable structures and chemistries, making them ideal candidates for filtration and adsorption applications. The AI platform rapidly explored an immense design space—approximately 300 trillion possible material structures—to identify over 5000 novel designs tailored for PFAS removal. This process, which typically takes years, was compressed into just six months, delivering promising candidates for further development and testing.

This industry-first partnership demonstrates the immense potential of AI in accelerating materials discovery. By precisely designing MOFs from scratch to meet specific industrial performance criteria, scientists can develop more stable, sustainable, and manufacturable solutions for critical environmental problems. The ability to target specific PFAS molecules, such as GenX, PFBS, and PFOS, at trace concentrations represents a major step forward in safeguarding water resources and public health.

The project is now advancing to the next phase of development, with further research already being scoped to build upon this success. This powerful synergy between AI and chemistry promises to unlock new avenues for addressing complex challenges across various industries, ushering in an era of more efficient and environmentally conscious chemical solutions.
