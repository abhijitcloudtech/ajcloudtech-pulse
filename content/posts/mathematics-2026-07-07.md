## Math's Latest Leap: Unveiling Nature's Hidden Equations with AI

**Philadelphia, PA – July 7, 2026** – In a significant development at the intersection of artificial intelligence and mathematics, researchers at Clarkson University have introduced KANDy, an innovative AI tool designed to uncover the underlying mathematical equations of complex and chaotic systems directly from observational data. This breakthrough, reported on July 6, 2026, promises to provide scientists with an unprecedented level of insight into phenomena previously difficult to model.

Traditional AI models often operate as "black boxes," adept at making predictions but offering little explanation as to *why* they arrive at those conclusions. KANDy, short for Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks for Dynamics, takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of merely predicting future actions, KANDy aims to identify the precise mathematical rules that govern a system's behavior.

This novel technology builds upon a class of neural networks known as Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs), adapting them specifically for dynamical systems. By feeding KANDy data from a complex physical system, the tool can then deduce the governing equations, succeeding even where existing equation-discovery methods have fallen short. For instance, KANDy has already demonstrated its ability to recover the topological structure of mathematical objects like the Hopf Fibration, showcasing its capacity to grasp deeper properties of intricate systems.

The ability to derive interpretable equations from noisy, nonlinear, and unpredictable data offers a powerful new avenue for understanding a vast array of scientific and engineering challenges. This development marks a crucial step toward "white box" AI in scientific discovery, providing not just answers, but also the mathematical language behind them.
