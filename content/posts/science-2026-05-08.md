**Chemistry Breakthrough Poised to Accelerate Drug Discovery**

May 08, 2026 – In a significant advance for medical science, chemists at the University of Chicago have unveiled a groundbreaking method that could dramatically speed up the development of new small-molecule drugs. Published on April 30 in *Science*, this discovery simplifies the complex process of customizing molecules by easily swapping carbon-oxygen pairs for nitrogen atoms.

Small-molecule drugs are a cornerstone of modern medicine, encompassing everything from common pain relievers like ibuprofen to treatments for cancer and eczema. Traditionally, creating these compounds for testing involves a long and intricate series of steps, often requiring 10 or more stages to synthesize a desired molecule.

The new technique developed by the UChicago team, however, can reduce this laborious process to just one or two steps. This efficiency gain is expected to be a powerful new tool for researchers, allowing them to more quickly assemble target molecules and test them for therapeutic potential. Such an acceleration in molecular customization holds immense promise for pharmaceutical research, potentially bringing new treatments to patients much faster.
