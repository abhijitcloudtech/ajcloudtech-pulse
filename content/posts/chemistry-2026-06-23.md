## AI Unleashes a New Era in Catalyst Discovery with "Flex-Cat" Lab

**June 23, 2026** – The pace of chemical innovation is set to accelerate dramatically with the unveiling of a groundbreaking "self-driving" chemistry laboratory designed to revolutionize catalyst discovery. Developed by researchers at North Carolina State University, the new platform, dubbed "Flex-Cat," autonomously navigates the complex landscape of catalyst recipes and reaction conditions, promising faster and more efficient pathways to essential industrial chemicals.

Catalysts are the unsung heroes of the chemical industry, silently driving countless reactions that produce everything from pharmaceuticals and plastics to fuels. However, discovering and optimizing new catalysts is traditionally a time-consuming and labor-intensive process, involving a vast experimental search space to find the right material, temperature, pressure, and concentration.

Flex-Cat addresses this challenge by integrating robotics, high-pressure chemical reactors, automated product analysis, and artificial intelligence. This intelligent system can autonomously screen hundreds of catalyst variations, not only identifying high-performing catalysts but also discovering "tunable" catalysts whose behavior can be adjusted to yield different products simply by altering reaction conditions. This ability to "steer" a reaction toward a desired product marks a significant leap forward, offering unprecedented control and efficiency in chemical synthesis.

The implications of Flex-Cat are vast. By accelerating the discovery of more selective and programmable catalysts, the platform could rapidly advance industries reliant on chemical transformations. From developing new life-saving drugs to creating more sustainable plastics and fuels, this autonomous lab heralds a future where chemical breakthroughs are achieved with unparalleled speed and precision.
