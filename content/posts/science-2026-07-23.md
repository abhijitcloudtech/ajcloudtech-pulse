## Cosmic Breath: Astronomers Confirm Atmosphere on Rocky Exoplanet in Habitable Zone

**July 23, 2026** – In a monumental leap for exoplanet research, astronomers have officially confirmed the existence of an atmosphere around LHS 1140 b, a rocky planet located within its star's habitable zone. This groundbreaking discovery, announced recently, marks a significant milestone in the ongoing quest to find life beyond Earth.

The planet, situated approximately 48 light-years away, has revealed its atmospheric veil through the tell-tale sign of helium slowly escaping into space. This finding provides the strongest evidence to date that rocky planets with Earth-like temperatures could exist beyond our solar system while retaining conditions potentially suitable for life.

An atmosphere is considered essential for a planet to support life as we understand it. The longevity of LHS 1140 b's atmosphere, estimated to have survived for over three billion years, makes it a prime candidate for future, more detailed observations. Scientists are eager to determine its full chemical composition and investigate the possibility of surface oceans or other habitability-linked features. This observation of helium leakage offers a validated model that could pave the way for discovering more rocky worlds with atmospheres.
