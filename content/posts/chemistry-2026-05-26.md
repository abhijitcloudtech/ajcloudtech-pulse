## AI Unleashes New Defense Against Forever Chemicals in Water

**May 26, 2026** – The fight against "forever chemicals" just got a powerful new ally: artificial intelligence. In a significant breakthrough, Kemira and CuspAI have leveraged generative AI to design novel materials specifically engineered to remove per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) from drinking and process water. This industry-first collaboration promises to revolutionize water purification and accelerate environmental remediation efforts.

PFAS, notorious for their persistence in the environment and potential health risks, pose a global challenge. Traditional methods for their removal are often energy-intensive or not fully effective at trace concentrations. This new approach harnesses the power of AI to rapidly develop targeted solutions.

The project saw CuspAI's platform explore an astonishing 300 trillion possible material structures, ultimately delivering over 5,000 novel designs of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) with detailed property data for priority PFAS molecules like GenX, PFBS, and PFOS. This process, which would typically take years, was compressed into just six months, leading to the selection of approximately 20 promising candidates now advancing to further development and testing.

This marks the first commercial partnership to apply generative AI end-to-end for PFAS remediation, designing entirely new structures from scratch against industrial performance criteria. The stable, sustainable, and manufacturable chemistry behind these AI-designed MOFs offers immense potential for ensuring cleaner water globally.
