## Breakthrough in Precision Medicine: New Chemistry Promises Safer Cancer Drugs

**Tampa, FL – July 23, 2026** – Today marks a significant stride in the fight against cancer, as researchers at Moffitt Cancer Center announce a pioneering development in covalent chemistry aimed at creating safer, more precise targeted therapies. This groundbreaking work could dramatically reduce the debilitating side effects associated with current cancer treatments and enhance their effectiveness.

Existing covalent drugs, while powerful in targeting cancer-driving proteins, often suffer from broad reactivity, leading to unintended interactions with healthy proteins. These off-target effects are a major cause of severe side effects, limiting the scope and safety of these vital medications.

The team, led by Justin M. Lopchuk, Ph.D., from Moffitt’s Drug Discovery Department, has engineered a novel chemical "warhead" centered on a small, highly strained molecular structure known as a bicyclobutane. This innovative design allows drugs to bind much more selectively to the target amino acid, cysteine, which is frequently implicated in cancer, while largely avoiding reactions with healthy proteins.

What makes this discovery particularly promising is the ability to integrate this new chemistry into existing drug candidates late in their development. Instead of designing entirely new drugs from scratch, scientists can now modify and improve established therapies, potentially accelerating the availability of safer treatments for patients. Early laboratory and animal studies have shown that drugs redesigned with this bicyclobutane warhead match the tumor-fighting efficacy of approved therapies but with significantly greater precision and no obvious signs of toxicity.

This advancement offers a clear path towards developing next-generation precision medicines, not only for cancer but potentially for a wide array of other diseases. The implications for patient well-being and the future of drug design are profound, promising a new era of highly effective and much gentler treatments.
