## Mathematics World Buzzes as Fields Medals Loom at ICM 2026

The mathematics community is abuzz with anticipation as the International Congress of Mathematicians (ICM) 2026 is set to kick off in Philadelphia this week, from July 23-30. A major highlight of this prestigious event, often dubbed the "Olympics of mathematics," will be the awarding of the coveted Fields Medals, recognizing outstanding mathematical achievement in individuals under 40. As of today, July 20, 2026, speculation is rife about who will be honored with this highest accolade in mathematics.

Prediction markets and expert discussions point to several leading contenders, with Jacob Tsimerman, Hong Wang, Yu Deng, and John Pardon frequently mentioned. Jacob Tsimerman, a professor at the University of Toronto, is widely recognized for his groundbreaking work, including the resolution of the André-Oort and Griffiths conjectures, significant milestones in modern number theory. Meanwhile, Hong Wang from NYU has garnered considerable attention for settling the three-dimensional Kakeya Conjecture in 2025, a problem that had stumped mathematicians for decades. Her achievement is considered one of the top breakthroughs of the 21st century.

The ICM 2026 is not just for mathematicians; it also includes a series of free public events from July 24-28 in downtown Philadelphia, offering talks and activities to engage a broader audience with the beauty and relevance of mathematics. This coincides with the United States celebrating 2026 as the "Year of Mathematics," further emphasizing the importance of mathematical sciences. The announcements next week will undoubtedly shape future research directions and celebrate the brilliant minds pushing the boundaries of human knowledge.
